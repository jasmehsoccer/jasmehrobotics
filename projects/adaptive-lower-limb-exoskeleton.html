Human-Centered Robotics

Adaptive Lower-Limb Exoskeleton

Investigating whether reinforcement learning can coordinate motor assistance with an electrically actuated passive spring system to reduce fatigue and improve mobility.

RL + Exoskeleton Assistance

Overview

A mobility-assistance project focused on adaptive control for wearable robotics.

Problem

Many elderly users experience fatigue and reduced mobility. Assistance should adapt to the user's motion instead of applying fixed support.

Architecture

Conceptual architecture combines a lower-limb exoskeleton, motor assistance, passive spring mechanics, sensing, and an RL-based controller.

Hardware

Lower-limb support structure, electrically actuated passive spring system, motor assistance, and wearable sensing considerations.

Software

Reinforcement learning controller designed to adapt assistance based on movement and fatigue-related signals.

Results

Active research direction and portfolio anchor for robotics that improves quality of life.

Lessons Learned

Human-centered robots require attention to safety, comfort, adaptation, and measurable impact.
